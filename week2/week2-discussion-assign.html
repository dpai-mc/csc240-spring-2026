üí¨ Discussion Prompt

After completing this week's reading and class activities, reflect on the following questions and share your thoughts:

1. Equivalence Relations in Practice

Think of an equivalence relation you've encountered in programming or databases. How does it partition data?

Example: Grouping students by grade level creates equivalence classes (e.g., all freshmen are equivalent).

2. Function Types in Computer Science

Can you identify a situation where you'd specifically want an **injective** function? A **surjective** function? A **bijective** function?

Think about: Data structures, encryption, hashing, unique identifiers, resource allocation.

3. The Pigeonhole Principle's Power

The pigeonhole principle seems simple, but it has powerful applications. Can you think of a scenario in computer science where this principle helps prove something must be true?

Consider: Hash collisions, data compression, network protocols, algorithm guarantees.
