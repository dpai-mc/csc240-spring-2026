Problem 1: Relation Properties (3 points)

Let A = {1, 2, 3, 4} and R = {(1,1), (1,2), (2,1), (2,2), (3,3), (4,4)}.

Determine whether R is:

  1. Reflexive (justify your answer)
  2. Symmetric (justify your answer)
  3. Transitive (justify your answer)
  4. Is R an equivalence relation? Why or why not?
Hint: For each property, refer to its formal definition. If a property does not hold, provide a counterexample.

Problem 2: Equivalence Relations (2 points)

Define a relation R on the set of all students at Mendocino College where (a,b) ‚àà R if students a and b are enrolled in at least one common course.

  1. Prove or disprove: R is reflexive
  2. Prove or disprove: R is symmetric
  3. Prove or disprove: R is transitive
  4. Is R an equivalence relation? Justify your answer.
Hint: Think about the real-world implications of these properties for students and courses. For transitivity, consider if A shares a class with B, and B shares a class with C, does A necessarily share a class with C?
