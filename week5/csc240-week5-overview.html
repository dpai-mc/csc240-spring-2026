3. Prerequisites from Weeks 3-4

🧠 Essential Concepts to Review

Before diving into Week 5, ensure you have a solid understanding of these topics from previous weeks:

  • Propositional logic and logical connectives (∧, ∨, ¬, →, ↔)
  • Truth tables and logical equivalences
  • Predicate logic with quantifiers (∀, ∃)
  • Contrapositive equivalence: $$(P \rightarrow Q) \equiv (\neg Q \rightarrow \neg P)$$
  • Negation of quantified statements

These logical foundations are crucial for understanding and constructing formal proofs.

4. Week 5 Modules at a Glance

This week's content is divided into 6 cohesive learning modules. Each module includes readings, examples, interactive activities, and practice problems.

Module 5.1: Introduction to Mathematical Proofs

⏱️ Estimated Time: 1.5 hours

This module sets the stage by defining what a mathematical proof is, why it's important in computer science, and outlining the essential components of any well-structured proof. You'll also review common theorem forms and their related logical structures (converse, inverse, contrapositive).

  • Explain the purpose and importance of mathematical proofs in computer science
  • Identify the components of a well-structured proof
  • Recognize common theorem forms and their logical structures
  • Distinguish between a theorem, its converse, inverse, and contrapositive

Module 5.2: Direct Proofs

⏱️ Estimated Time: 2 hours

You'll learn the most fundamental proof technique: direct proof. This involves assuming the hypothesis of a conditional statement and logically deriving its conclusion using definitions, axioms, and previously established theorems. Examples will focus on parity, divisibility, and rational numbers.

  • Apply the direct proof technique to prove conditional statements
  • Use formal definitions to translate assumptions into mathematical expressions
  • Construct logical chains of reasoning from hypothesis to conclusion
  • Write clear, well-structured direct proofs for statements involving parity, divisibility, and rational numbers

Module 5.3: Proof by Cases

⏱️ Estimated Time: 1.5 hours

This module introduces a technique for when your argument naturally splits into several distinct scenarios. You'll learn how to divide a problem into exhaustive and mutually exclusive cases, proving the conclusion holds for each case separately. This is particularly useful for problems involving absolute values or modular arithmetic.

  • Recognize when proof by cases is the appropriate technique
  • Partition a domain into exhaustive, mutually exclusive cases
  • Construct complete proofs by cases with proper structure
  • Apply proof by cases to problems involving parity, absolute values, and inequalities

Module 5.4: Proof by Contrapositive

⏱️ Estimated Time: 1.5 hours

Building on logical equivalences, this module explores proving a conditional statement $$P \rightarrow Q$$ by instead proving its contrapositive $$\neg Q \rightarrow \neg P$$. This technique is often more straightforward when directly proving P implies Q is difficult, especially when the conclusion is easier to negate.

  • Explain why a statement and its contrapositive are logically equivalent
  • Recognize when proof by contrapositive is advantageous over direct proof
  • Correctly form the contrapositive of a given conditional statement
  • Apply the contrapositive technique to prove mathematical theorems

Module 5.5: Proof by Counterexample

⏱️ Estimated Time: 1.5 hours

Not all statements are true! This module teaches you how to disprove a universal statement ($$\forall x, P(x)$$) by finding a single, specific instance (a counterexample) where the statement fails. This technique is analogous to finding bugs through testing in computer science.

  • Explain when counterexamples are appropriate for disproving statements
  • Construct effective counterexamples to disprove false universal statements
  • Apply systematic strategies for finding counterexamples
  • Distinguish between proving and disproving statements

Module 5.6: Mathematical Writing and Proof Style

⏱️ Estimated Time: 1 hour

The final module focuses on the craft of writing proofs. You'll learn the "4 C's" (Clarity, Completeness, Correctness, Conciseness), standard conventions, common errors to avoid, and how to effectively revise your proofs to ensure they are convincing and easy to understand.

  • Write clear, well-organized mathematical proofs
  • Use proper notation, terminology, and conventions
  • Identify and correct common proof writing errors
  • Connect proof techniques to algorithm correctness arguments

9. Looking Ahead to Week 6

🚀 Week 6: Proof Techniques II

Next week, you'll delve into more advanced and powerful proof techniques:

  • Proof by Contradiction: A method where you assume the negation of what you want to prove and show it leads to a logical inconsistency.
  • Mathematical Induction: A crucial technique for proving statements about natural numbers, especially relevant for analyzing recursive algorithms and data structures in computer science.
  • Strong Induction: A variation of induction that provides a broader assumption in the inductive step.

The strong foundation in proof writing you've built this week will be invaluable for these new topics!
