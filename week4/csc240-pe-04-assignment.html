What You'll Build

Implement a program that converts logical expressions to Conjunctive Normal Form (CNF) or Disjunctive Normal Form (DNF) and simplifies expressions using logical equivalences. This assignment reinforces your understanding of normal forms and equivalence laws from Modules 4.1 and 4.2.

üéØ Learning Objectives

This assignment helps you achieve:

  • Convert propositions to CNF and DNF
  • Apply De Morgan's laws and other logical equivalences
  • Implement algorithmic transformations of logical expressions
  • Maps to CSLO 1: Apply symbolic logic and proof methods to model and analyze program/algorithm correctness

üìù Requirements

Core Features (Required)

  • Input Parsing: Accept logical expressions with variables and operators
  • Supported Operators:
    • AND: ‚àß or && or AND
    • OR: ‚à® or || or OR
    • NOT: ¬¨ or ! or NOT
    • Implication: ‚Üí or -> or IMPLIES
    • Biconditional: ‚Üî or <-> or IFF
  • CNF Conversion: Convert any valid expression to Conjunctive Normal Form
  • DNF Conversion: Convert any valid expression to Disjunctive Normal Form
  • Simplification: Apply equivalence laws to simplify the result
  • Output: Display the converted expression in readable format

Conversion Algorithm Requirements

Your program must implement these steps:

  1. Eliminate implications and biconditionals:
    • P ‚Üí Q ‚â° ¬¨P ‚à® Q
    • P ‚Üî Q ‚â° (P ‚Üí Q) ‚àß (Q ‚Üí P)
  2. Move negations inward using De Morgan's Laws:
    • ¬¨(P ‚àß Q) ‚â° ¬¨P ‚à® ¬¨Q
    • ¬¨(P ‚à® Q) ‚â° ¬¨P ‚àß ¬¨Q
    • ¬¨¬¨P ‚â° P
  3. Apply distribution laws:
    • For CNF: Distribute OR over AND
    • For DNF: Distribute AND over OR
  4. Simplify using equivalence laws:
    • Identity, Domination, Idempotent laws
    • Remove tautologies and contradictions
