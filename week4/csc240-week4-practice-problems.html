üìñ Reference Guide

Quantifier Translation Patterns

English Pattern Symbolic Form Key Connective
All P's are Q's ‚àÄx (P(x) ‚Üí Q(x)) ‚Üí (Implication)
Some P's are Q's ‚àÉx (P(x) ‚àß Q(x)) ‚àß (Conjunction)
No P's are Q's ‚àÄx (P(x) ‚Üí ¬¨Q(x)) ‚Üí (Implication)
Not all P's are Q's ‚àÉx (P(x) ‚àß ¬¨Q(x)) ‚àß (Conjunction)

De Morgan's Laws for Quantifiers

Original Statement Negation
$$ \neg(\forall x \, P(x)) $$ $$ \equiv \exists x \, \neg P(x) $$
$$ \neg(\exists x \, P(x)) $$ $$ \equiv \forall x \, \neg P(x) $$

Combine these with propositional De Morgan's laws: $$ \neg(P \land Q) \equiv \neg P \lor \neg Q $$ and $$ \neg(P \lor Q) \equiv \neg P \land \neg Q $$.

Also recall: $$ \neg(P \to Q) \equiv P \land \neg Q $$ and $$ \neg \neg P \equiv P $$.
