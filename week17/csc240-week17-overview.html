Week 17 of 17

Final Project Completion and Submission

CSC 240 – Discrete Structures • Spring 2026

Welcome to Week 17 — the final week of CSC 240!

This week is your course capstone. There is no new conceptual content introduced. Instead, all of your energy goes toward integrating, testing, documenting, and submitting the Final Project: Network Analysis Application.

You have spent Weeks 12–16 building the knowledge and code that power this project. Week 17 is where it all comes together into a single, professional-quality deliverable.

This Week's Theme

Week 17 is dedicated to integration, synthesis, and delivery. You will assemble the graph algorithms, tree-based data structures, and complexity analysis skills you developed in Weeks 12–16 into a cohesive Network Analysis Application. The focus is on making all four project components work together correctly, conducting runtime experiments, writing a clear analysis document, polishing your code to professional standards, and submitting a complete software package. This is not a week of learning new theory — it is a week of demonstrating mastery through application.

Learning Goals

By the end of Week 17, you will be able to:

  • Integrate graph construction, traversal, MST, and shortest-path algorithms into a single working application
  • Implement a student-built tree-based data structure (min-heap/priority queue or BST) that meaningfully supports a graph algorithm
  • Produce a runtime comparison of algorithms on at least two different graph sizes, interpreting results in terms of Big-O complexity
  • Write a clear analysis document that explains algorithm choices, compares BFS vs. DFS applicability, provides time/space complexity analysis, and connects the application to a real-world scenario
  • Deliver professional-quality software deliverables: clean code, README, test cases, and documentation
  • Reflect on your own learning and development process through a self-evaluation form

Required Activities

Complete the following four learning modules in order, then submit your Final Project:

  1. Module 17.1 Integration Testing and Component Verification
  2. Module 17.2 Runtime Comparison and Complexity Analysis
  3. Module 17.3 Analysis Document and Technical Writing
  4. Module 17.4 Code Quality, Documentation, and Final Submission
  5. Final Project Submission Final Project: Network Analysis Application (submit via Canvas Assignments)

The Final Project (250 points, 25% of your course grade) is the sole graded deliverable this week. There is no PE, PS, discussion, or quiz.

Time Management Reminder

Week 17 is the highest-effort week of the second half of the course, estimated at approximately 10 hours of focused work. Plan your schedule accordingly.

If you followed the course guidance and treated PE-09 (Week 14) as your project scaffold — and made progress on your tree-based structure during Week 15 — you should be entering this week in the testing, documenting, and polishing phase, not the initial coding phase. Use the four modules above to guide your workflow systematically from integration testing through final submission.

Looking Ahead

Week 17 is the final week of CSC 240 – Discrete Structures. Once you submit your Final Project, you will have completed the course. Congratulations on the work you have put in this semester — from sets and logic through graphs, trees, and algorithms. Best of luck on your submission!
